ABOUT KAWASAKI

Kawasaki Ninja 1000SX – Opinions & Breakdown 1. Engine & Performance 1,043cc inline-four, tuned for smooth, usable power (around 140 hp). Not as raw as the ZX-10R superbike, but way more practical for real-world riding. Strong mid-range torque makes it effortless for highway pulls and overtakes. 2. Comfort & Practicality More upright seating than pure sportbikes → great for longer rides and commuting. Fairing and windscreen give solid wind protection. Designed as a sport-tourer → you can ride fast but still stay comfortable. 3. Electronics & Features Comes with modern rider aids: Ride modes Traction control Cornering ABS Quick shifter (on newer models) Large TFT display and Bluetooth smartphone connectivity in latest versions. 4. Design Classic Kawasaki Ninja look, but more “grown-up” than the ZX-10R. Aggressive enough to look sporty, but not as intimidating as the H2 or ZX-10R. 5. Pros & Cons Pros Versatile: sporty + comfortable Strong mid-range engine (not just top-end power) Good for both city and touring Affordable compared to superbikes Cons Heavier than ZX-10R (so less nimble on track) Not as extreme if you want a pure race bike Stock exhaust note is a bit muted compared to rivals

Top speed is approximately 152.8 mph (246 km/h) .
